One example, two languages

The Lotka-Voltera model:

\begin{aligned} {\frac {dx}{dt}}&=\alpha x-\beta xy,\\{\frac {dy}{dt}}&= -\delta y-\gamma xy, \end{aligned}

where,

  • $x$ is the number of prey,
  • $y$ is the number of predator,
  • $\alpha$, $\beta$, $\gamma$, $\delta$ parameters describing the interaction between the species.

DifferentialEquations.jl solves equation you haven't even heard of ...

  • Discrete equations (function maps, discrete stochastic simulations
  • Ordinary differential equations (ODEs)
  • Split and Partitioned ODEs (Symplectic integrators, IMEX Methods)
  • Stochastic ordinary differential equations (SODEs or SDEs)
  • Random differential equations (RODEs or RDEs)
  • Differential algebraic equations (DAEs)
  • Delay differential equations (DDEs)
  • Mixed discrete and continuous equations (Hybrid Equations, Jump Diffusions)
  • (Stochastic) partial differential equations

... And is Jam-packed with other features

  • Forward and adjoint local sensitivity analysis for fast gradient computations
  • Optimization-based and Bayesian parameter estimation
  • Neural differential equations with DiffEqFlux.jl
  • Automatic distributed, multithreaded, and GPU parallelism of ensemble trajectories
  • Global sensitivity analysis
  • Uncertainty quantification
  • ...
