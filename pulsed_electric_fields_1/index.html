There are 27 equations in the model, here are three of them...

Nernst-Planck equation (solution):

$$\displaystyle J_{i,x}= - D_i\, \cfrac{\delta c_i}{\delta x} + \cfrac{j\, t_i}{z_i \, F}$$

Solved for Na+, Cl- & DS-

Kedem-Katchalsky equation (membrane):

$$\displaystyle J_{x}= - P_m\, \cfrac{\delta c}{\delta x} + \cfrac{j\, t_{c,m}}{z \, F}$$

$$\displaystyle P_m = \cfrac{2 \, R \, T \, \kappa_m (1-t_{c,m})t_{app, m} \, g}{F^2\, c}$$

Solved for NaCl as a whole (No SDS)

Conclusions and perspectives

Conclusions:

  • PEF can improve the fouling behaviour
  • But choosing the right frequency is essential!
    • Low frequencies are counterproductive
    • High frequencies are also counterproductive (galvanostatic)
  • There is an important trade-off with the energy consumption

Perspectives:

  • Including fouling in the energy efficiency
    • Predictive model for fouling resistance required
  • System optimisation
